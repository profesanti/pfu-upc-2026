## Full call text (English)

### Objective

A total of 99 FPU-UPC grants are announced to promote the training of researchers through the awarding of grants for the recruitment of predoctoral research staff for the year 2026, enabling full-time dedication to studies leading to the award of the PhD degree.

As a new feature of this call, two rounds will be held: one at the end of the second semester of the 2025–26 academic year and another at the beginning of the 2026–27 academic year.

### General requirements

To be admitted to the selection process, applicants must meet the following requirements at the time of acceptance of the grant:

- Be admitted to a doctoral programme with a commitment to enrol in thesis supervision in the 2026–27 academic year, or hold conditional admission to a doctoral programme. For the first round, applicants must undertake to formalise enrolment when the registration period opens (approximately September/October 2026). Failure to do so will result in revocation of the grant.
- Applicants may also participate if they are already enrolled in academic supervision of a doctoral programme in the previous academic year at the time of grant acceptance. Doctoral programmes must belong to the UPC.
- Not have benefited from any other grant of a similar nature (predoctoral contract) with a duration exceeding 12 months at the end of the application period.
- Not hold a PhD degree.
- Not have benefited, in whole or in part, from this grant in previous calls.
- Have the endorsement of a UPC faculty member acting as thesis supervisor, with accredited research experience and a permanent link to the UPC or an employment contract exceeding the four-year duration of the grant. In the case of co-supervision, at least one supervisor must meet this requirement. A thesis supervisor may not supervise or co-supervise more than one FPU, nor supervise a beneficiary from previous FPU-UPC calls while the grant remains in force, except in duly justified cases assessed by the Vice-Rector for Faculty Policy.
- Hold accreditation of sufficient knowledge of Catalan at C1 CEFR level. Applicants who do not hold this accreditation must request a temporary exemption in accordance with Agreement CG/2023/03/09 of the UPC Governing Council.

### Grant duration

- The predoctoral contract shall have a minimum duration of one year and may be renewed annually under this programme up to a maximum of four years.

### Characteristics

- A total of 99 FPI-UPC 2026 grants are announced and will be distributed among departments, institutes and UNESCO Chairs, in accordance with the document available in the “Documentation and support” section.
- Research staff in training should collaborate in teaching activities for up to a maximum of 180 hours over the duration of the predoctoral contract, without exceeding 60 hours per year. Agreement CG/2024/05/12 of 25 October 2024 regulates remuneration for teaching activities carried out under predoctoral training contracts.

### Amount of the grant

- €20,063.96 gross per year in the first and second year, and €25,079.88 gross per year in the third and fourth year.
- In addition, all beneficiaries of the FPI-UPC 2025 programme are entitled to free enrolment in doctoral programme supervision, with a maximum of four free enrolments for the entire grant period. Fees for academic record management are not eligible.

### Submission of applications

Through the UPC electronic headquarters ([original link](https://seuelectronica.upc.edu/ca/tots-els-tramits/convocatoria-ajuts-formacio-professorat-universitari-FPU)), by completing the application form “Call for university faculty training grants (FPU-UPC 2026) for the completion of a doctoral thesis at the UPC”.

- Applicants may submit only one application.
- Each thesis supervisor may appear in only one application and must sign an endorsement document, which the applicant must attach to the application form: [`fpu-upc-2026/aval-director-de-tesi-fpu-upc-2026.docx`](./fpu-upc-2026/aval-director-de-tesi-fpu-upc-2026.docx).
- The application must indicate the department, institute or UNESCO Chair under which the application is submitted, chosen from among the 41 available options.

To benefit from the grant, successful applicants must hold the qualifications granting access to doctoral studies at the time of formalisation of the contract.

Documents **mandatory** to be submitted with the application form, always in PDF format:

- Curriculum Vitae of the applicant, and Curriculum vitae of the thesis supervisor, if applicable.
- Copy of the doctoral programme admission letter or, where applicable, provisional admission letter.
- Endorsement document from the applicant’s doctoral thesis supervisor (use: [`fpu-upc-2026/aval-director-de-tesi-fpu-upc-2026.docx`](./fpu-upc-2026/aval-director-de-tesi-fpu-upc-2026.docx)). Each supervisor may endorse only one application.
- Copy of academic transcripts for degrees not completed at the UPC, where applicable. Including the date of degree completion and the grade point average. In the case of studies completed outside the Spanish university system, if the academic transcript does not include a description of the university’s grading system, documentation certifying this system must be attached, indicating the maximum and minimum grades, and the minimum passing grade.
- Copy of the equivalence certificate of the average grade for degrees obtained at foreign institutions, issued through the Ministry portal: https://universidades.sede.gob.es/pagina/index/directorio/Equivalencia_notas_medias/language/ca_ES.
- **Important:** In order for the departments/institutes/UNESCO chairs to evaluate the applications they receive, according to their prioritization criteria related to Annex B.2), applicants must complete the annexed document/work plan and upload it to the corresponding section in the online application portal (PDF format): [`fpu-upc-2026/document-annex_ajuts-fpu-upc_2026.docx`](./fpu-upc-2026/document-annex_ajuts-fpu-upc_2026.docx).
- Copy of the certificate accrediting sufficient knowledge of Catalan at C1 CEFR level. Applicants without this certificate may request a temporary exemption via the standardised application form.

Additional document to be submitted:

- For the calculation of the weighted average grade, applicants must submit an official document indicating the average grade of graduates from their cohort. This document is not required for degrees awarded by the UPC and must be attached under “other annexes”. In the absence of this document, only the candidate's average mark will be taken into account.

### Department-specific note (Matemàtiques / MAT, code 749)

For advisors/candidates applying through the Mathematics department:

- In the grant distribution annex (`no-ajuts.pdf`), **MAT (749) has 3 grants**, all in the **1st edition**.
- In the prioritisation criteria annex (`criteris-davaluacio.pdf`), section **“749 Matemàtiques (MAT)”** defines the department-level rules.
- MAT scoring is split into:
  - **Section A (academic record): 50%**
  - **Section B (department ad hoc committee evaluation): 50%**
- MAT-specific points include:
  - weighted academic grade threshold criterion with **minimum 7/10**
  - **CVA of the thesis supervisor** is explicitly evaluated
  - **minimum admissible final score: 7/10**

This confirms there is effectively a department-level “pre-call”/internal prioritisation for MAT within the global FPU call.

### MAT application checklist (advisor + candidate)

For a MAT-based application, prepare at least the following:

**Candidate**
- Submit the application through the UPC e-headquarters form (official link in the call).
- CV in PDF (mandatory). In the annex/work plan template, include the candidate curriculum section (**max 3 pages**).
- Doctoral admission (or provisional admission) letter.
- Academic transcripts (and, for foreign studies, grade-equivalence and grading-scale documentation).
- Annex/work plan document completed and uploaded in PDF.
- Catalan C1 certificate (or temporary exemption request, if applicable).

**Advisor (thesis supervisor)**
- Sign the official endorsement document (`aval-director-de-tesi-fpu-upc-2026.docx`) for this application.
- Ensure the supervisor appears in only one application.
- Provide supervisor CV in PDF; for MAT evaluation, prepare a strong **CVA-style** version because MAT criteria explicitly assess the supervisor CVA.

**MAT-specific strategy checks**
- Verify candidate weighted grade is competitive and at/above the MAT threshold (criterion includes minimum 7/10).
- Align the work plan with MAT section-B criteria (trajectory, thesis topic, 4-year plan, suitability, and group research context).
- Target final prioritisation score above the MAT admissibility minimum (**7/10**).
